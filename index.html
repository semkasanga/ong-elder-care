AGED-CARE FOUNDATION is a registered Zambian non-profit dedicated to the welfare, health, and social inclusion of vulnerable older persons. We work alongside communities and partners to ensure no elder is left behind.

Who We Are

Champions for Zambia's Most Vulnerable Elders

AGED-CARE FOUNDATION is a registered non-profit and charitable organization established to address the growing challenges faced by vulnerable elderly persons — including poverty, neglect, poor health, social isolation, and abuse. We partner with communities, healthcare institutions, government agencies, and the private sector to deliver sustainable, evidence-based support.

  • Poverty
  • Neglect & abandonment
  • Poor health
  • Social isolation
  • Abuse
  • Inadequate support services
We recognize older persons as custodians of knowledge, culture, and community values.

Our Purpose

Mission, Vision & Values

Guided by compassion and accountability, we work to ensure every older person in Zambia can age with dignity, security, and belonging.

Our Mission

To improve the quality of life of vulnerable older persons through care services, health interventions, advocacy, research, empowerment programmes, and strategic partnerships.

Our Vision

A Zambia where every older person lives with dignity, good health, protection, and social inclusion.

Our Strategic Goal

To become Zambia's leading organization promoting the welfare, protection, health, and social inclusion of vulnerable older persons by 2030.

Dignity First

Every older person deserves respect, security, and a life lived with honour.

Compassionate Care

We deliver practical, human-centered support with empathy and presence.

Social Inclusion

No elderly person should be isolated, forgotten, or left behind.

Transparency & Accountability

Strong governance, financial stewardship, and measurable impact guide everything we do.

Community-Driven Action

We work alongside communities, caregivers, and partners to create lasting change.

Evidence-Based Impact

Research, monitoring, and evaluation ensure our programmes deliver real results.

What We Do

Seven Pillars of Compassionate Action

Our strategic programmes address the full spectrum of needs faced by vulnerable older persons — from daily essentials to advocacy and institutional support.

Elderly Welfare & Livelihood Support

Food, clothing, hygiene kits, emergency relief, and home visitations for vulnerable elders — ensuring daily needs are met with dignity and compassion.

Learn More

Health & Wellbeing

Health screenings, psychosocial support, caregiver training, and active ageing initiatives that promote healthy, dignified lives for older persons.

Learn More

Advocacy, Rights & Inclusion

Awareness campaigns, elder abuse prevention, policy advocacy, and age-friendly community building to protect and empower older persons.

Learn More

Institutional Support

Hospital visitation and correctional facility support for elderly patients and inmates — a unique focus area providing comfort, care, and reintegration.

Learn More

Research & Knowledge

Evidence-based programming through research, policy analysis, needs assessments, and published reports on ageing in Zambia.

Learn More

Partnerships & Mobilisation

Grants, corporate partnerships, individual giving, and international collaboration to sustain and scale our impact across Zambia.

Learn More

Governance & Strength

Accountable governance, volunteer development, staff capacity building, and organizational excellence for long-term sustainability.
